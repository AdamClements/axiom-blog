**RECOVERED DOCUMENT — UNCLASSIFIED**
*Origin: Unknown field station. Found in ammunition crate, handwritten on requisition forms.*

---

Everything stopped.

Not gradually. Not a rolling blackout. One moment the displays were up, comms were live, the whole network humming along like it always did. Then a flash — not light exactly, more like a pressure change — and everything went quiet.

I don't mean the radios went down. I mean *everything*. The fluorescents. The coffee machine. Jensen's watch. The fire suppression panel. The card reader on the armoury door. Dead. All of it. Simultaneously.

We stood in the dark for what felt like five minutes before someone found a torch that worked. Mechanical switch, incandescent bulb. The LED ones were dead.

The vehicles wouldn't start. The backup generator wouldn't turn over — the ignition control board was fried. The landlines were dead. Not "no signal" dead. The handset itself was dead. The chip in the handset.

Liao said it was an EMP. Military grade, she said, but nothing military she'd ever seen. She said the pulse energy required to fry components this small, at this range — it wasn't a weapon. It was a decision. Someone decided that everything with a transistor below a certain size needed to stop existing.

She was right about the size threshold. We figured that out on day three.
