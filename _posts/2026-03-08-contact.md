We lost Scout 2 today.

The drone was running a sweep pattern eight clicks north when it stopped transmitting. Liao pulled the last data from the radio buffer: a scanner return showing three contacts at close range, transponder codes we'd never seen. Then silence.

We sent Scout 3 on a wide approach. It found the wreckage. Scout 2's chassis was intact but the CPU had taken a direct hit — a clean shot, precise, from a weapon system. The Griffin UNassembler. We know because Ironhorse printed the name on the barrel housing and the barrel housing was lying in the wreckage of our drone.

The contacts were still in the area. Scout 3's scanner picked them up: three mobile units, moving in a loose formation, transponders broadcasting on a frequency we hadn't assigned. They were running patrol patterns. Not random movement — coordinated, overlapping fields of fire, covering each other's blind spots.

These are the remnants. The drones that survived the pulse with their analog cores partially intact. They've been scavenging too. They've rebuilt themselves from wreckage, the same way we have, using the same Axiom and Ironhorse and Griffin components. But their firmware isn't written by humans. It's whatever fragmented neural code survived — jagged, unpredictable, but functional enough to move, scan, and shoot.

They don't negotiate. They don't respond to radio hails. They execute whatever remains of their last objective, and their last objective was combat.

Liao is already writing new firmware. Combat firmware. IFF targeting — check the transponder code, if it doesn't match ours, engage. She's looking up the GUN port addresses in the manual, working out range calculations, writing targeting loops in assembly.

She says we need more units. More CPUs. More iron.

Martinez is planning a scavenging run to a crash site twelve clicks east. Heavy drone wreckage — should be motors, armour, maybe a couple of intact processors. We'll need a hauler to bring the parts back. We'll need an armed escort for the hauler. We'll need firmware for both.

I'm learning assembly. Liao can't write it all herself. She gave me the manual and told me to start with chapter one. MOV, ADD, SUB. Load a value, store a value. One instruction at a time.

The CRT cursor blinks at me.

I start typing.
